Great White Sharks

The Great White (carcharodon carcharias) are known to be one of the world's largest predatory fish; measuring on average 15ft in length. They're mainly known for their amazing cameos in horror and suspence movies such as: Steven Spielberg's "Jaws," Johannes Roberts' "47 Meters Down," and more. While these film may depict these creatures as man-eating beasts, In actuality, they rarely attack humans. In cases where humans have been attacked by a Great White, under 100 documented cases in total, the Shark is less of a blood-thirsty monster and more curious or have mistaken surfers for their desired prey, seals. The average diet of a Great White Shark consist of: fish, Marine mammal (seals, sea lion and sometimes whales), sea turtles, and seabirds. A Great Whites' stratagy for attaining their prey is one known to most. Deep below the surface, it waits for its time to strike, when ready it will use its strength to immerge from the depth at a top speed of 35 mph, and often times fly out of the water, known as a breach. On Average these fish can live up to 70 years and live in coastal waters; with a high concentration in USA, South Africa, Japan, Oceania, Chile, and the Mediterranean.
